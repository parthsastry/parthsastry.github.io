Hi! I am a first-year Engineering and Applied Sciences Doctoral Student at the University of Massachusetts - Dartmouth. I'm associated with the Center for Scientific Computing & Visualization Research (CSCVR) and am currently working with Prof. Robert Fisheron Magneto-Rotational Instabilities in Accretion Disks.

professional interests

I am deeply interested in Astrophysical Fluid Dynamics, and especially want to explore how one can use the concept of LCSs (Lagrangian Coherent Structures) over stellar/galactic length and time scales to predict how gas clouds and stellar clusters evolve.

I have had experience with research on adapting fluid flow models to distinctly non-fluid problems, such as modelling movement of animals in a collective as a fluid flow. I have recently been working on time series analysis of atomic clock frequency data to predict end-of-life of these standards, and have also worked previously on developing statistical and machine learning models for predicting loan defualts using financial datasets.

un-professional interests

When not working or studying, I can usually be found reading fantasy or science fiction novels. I play the guitar as well, and a little pet project of mine is adapting post-rock music to the acoustic guitar.
